A message from Selina Esteves
June is National Indigenous History Month and June 21st, the first day of summer, marks National Indigenous Peoples Day. Sadly, we recently saw devastating news regarding the discovery of the remains of 215 Indigenous children in Kamloops, B.C. Please read President Gertler’s statement below. Along with the President, we acknowledge the dignity of each one of these 215 children, their families, and their communities.

We have also been shocked by the senseless killing of four members of a Muslim family, enjoying an evening walk in London, Ontario. We stand with President Gertler’s statement that Islamophobia as well as all forms of hate and violence have no place in our communities or in Canadian society.

Additionally, June marks Pride Month and the end of a challenging year for our graduates. The need to acknowledge past ills and emphatically embrace diversity, equity and inclusion for all people and to try and create safe spaces and environments that lift people to new heights is our pathway for doing better in the future.

Read the full statement from President Meric Gertler.


Selina Esteves
